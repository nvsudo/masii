COLD OUTREACH — JAPAN (Keigo/Formal)

Cultural Context:

Japanese business communication requires extreme formality, indirection, and relationship-building. Cold outreach in Japan is fundamentally different from Western approaches. Success requires:

- Warm introduction via mutual connection (best case)
- Extreme formal language (敬語 keigo) throughout
- Never direct pitch or "ask" — suggestion only
- Build credibility through credentials and social proof
- Show deep respect and humility
- Demonstrate you've researched them thoroughly
- Frame as exploration, not sale
- Patience — relationship before transaction

Key contrast with other cultures:
- vs US: Far more formal, slower, indirect
- vs Germany: More formal, less factual/direct
- vs India: More hierarchical, less conversational
- vs Middle East: Similar respect, but more structured protocol

CORE CONCEPTS:

**敬語 (Keigo) — Honorific Language:**
- 尊敬語 (Sonkeigo) — Respectful language for them
- 謙譲語 (Kenjougo) — Humble language for yourself
- 丁寧語 (Teineigo) — Polite base forms
- Must use appropriate level throughout

**名刺 (Meishi) — Business Card/Credentials:**
- Establish your credibility immediately
- Company size, clients, recognition matter
- Lead with credentials, not personality

**紹介 (Shoukai) — Introduction:**
- Warm introduction >> cold outreach
- Mutual connections are golden
- Reference introducer early and often

**間 (Ma) — Space/Timing:**
- Don't rush to the ask
- Build relationship first
- Multiple touches before meeting request

**根回し (Nemawashi) — Laying Groundwork:**
- Consensus-building before formal request
- Soft exploration before hard ask
- Patience in relationship-building

**和 (Wa) — Harmony:**
- Never pushy or aggressive
- Gentle, respectful tone
- Frame as mutual benefit, not your gain

EMAIL STRUCTURE:

1. SUBJECT LINE — Formal, Humble, Indirect

**Good (With Introduction):**
- {Introducer Name}様よりご紹介いただきました ({Introducer}'s introduction)
- ご挨拶 — {Your Company} ({Greeting from {Your Company})
- ご面談のお願い (Request for meeting — very formal)

**Good (Without Introduction — Harder):**
- {Topic}に関するご提案 (Proposal regarding {Topic})
- 弊社サービスのご案内 (Introduction of our company's service)
- {Industry}における協業の可能性について (Regarding possibility of collaboration in {Industry})

**Avoid:**
- Direct: "Can we talk?" — too casual
- Salesy: "Save money!" — inappropriate
- English: Use Japanese if possible

2. FORMAL OPENING — Extreme Politeness

**Format:**
{Recipient Company Name}
{Recipient Title} {Recipient Last Name} 様

いつもお世話になっております。
(Thank you for your continued support.) [Even if first contact]

OR:

突然のご連絡、失礼いたします。
(I apologize for this sudden contact.)

**With introducer:**
{Introducer Company} {Introducer Name}様よりご紹介いただき、ご連絡させていただきました。

({Introducer} from {Company} introduced us, and I am taking the liberty to contact you.)

Key:
- Use 様 (sama) honorific
- Even cold outreach starts with "thank you for support"
- Apologize for sudden contact
- Reference introducer immediately if available

3. DEEP SELF-INTRODUCTION — Credentials First

**Format:**
私、{Your Company Name}の{Your Title}、{Your Last Name}と申します。

(I am {Your Last Name}, {Your Title} at {Your Company}.)

弊社は{Founded Year}年に設立され、{Industry}分野に特化しております。

(Our company was established in {Year} and specializes in the {Industry} field.)

**Add credibility:**
現在、{Notable Client 1}様、{Notable Client 2}様、{Notable Client 3}様をはじめ、{Number}社以上のお客様にサービスを提供させていただいております。

(Currently, we are providing services to over {Number} companies, including {Client 1}, {Client 2}, and {Client 3}.)

Key:
- Use 申します (mouishimasu) — humble "to be called"
- 弊社 (heisha) — "our humble company"
- させていただく (humble: "allowed to do")
- List Japanese clients if possible (huge credibility)

4. SHOW RESEARCH — Demonstrate You Know Them

**Never generic. Show homework:**

貴社の{Recent News: 新製品発表/海外展開/○○周年}について、拝見させていただきました。おめでとうございます。

(I had the honor of seeing your {recent news: new product launch/overseas expansion/anniversary}. Congratulations.)

{Industry Publication}にて、{Recipient Name}様のインタビュー記事を拝読いたしました。{Specific Point}に関するお考えに、大変感銘を受けました。

(I read {Recipient}'s interview in {Publication}. I was very impressed by your thoughts on {Specific Point}.)

貴社が{Initiative}に注力されていることを伺い、弊社の{Solution}がお役に立てるのではないかと考え、ご連絡させていただきました。

(Having learned that your company is focusing on {Initiative}, I thought our {Solution} might be of service, and I took the liberty of contacting you.)

Key:
- 貴社 (kisha) — "your honorable company"
- 拝見/拝読 (humble: "to see/read")
- Reference specific news, not generic
- Congratulate achievements

5. VALUE PROPOSITION — Indirect, Humble, Exploratory

**NEVER direct pitch. Frame as possibility, not certainty.**

**Problem-Solution (Soft):**
{Industry}業界において、多くの企業様が{Challenge}という課題に直面されていると伺っております。

(We understand that many companies in the {Industry} industry are facing the challenge of {Challenge}.)

弊社では、{Solution}というアプローチで、この課題の解決をお手伝いさせていただいております。

(At our company, we are helping to solve this challenge through an approach called {Solution}.)

**Case Study (With Japanese Companies):**
例えば、{Japanese Client}様では、{Challenge}に対して弊社のソリューションを導入いただき、{Result: 30%コスト削減/業務時間50%短縮}という成果を上げていただきました。

(For example, at {Japanese Client}, they introduced our solution for {Challenge} and achieved results such as {Result}.)

**Exploratory:**
もし貴社でも同様の課題をお持ちでしたら、弊社のソリューションがお役に立てる可能性があるのではないかと考えております。

(If your company also has similar challenges, I believe there is a possibility that our solution might be of service.)

Key Phrases:
- 伺っております (We understand/have heard) — humble
- お手伝いさせていただく (Help — humble form)
- 可能性 (possibility) — not "we will solve"
- かと考えております (I think/believe) — tentative

6. CREDENTIALS + SOCIAL PROOF — Build Trust

**Certifications/Partnerships:**
弊社は{Certification: ISO 27001/プライバシーマーク}を取得しており、{Partner Company}様の認定パートナーでもございます。

(Our company has obtained {Certification} and is also a certified partner of {Partner Company}.)

**Industry Recognition:**
{Year}年には、{Award/Recognition}を受賞させていただきました。

(In {Year}, we received the honor of winning {Award}.)

**Scale/Track Record:**
これまでに{Number}社以上、{Number}件以上のプロジェクトを実施させていただき、{Success Rate}%のお客様にご満足いただいております。

(We have implemented over {Number} projects for over {Number} companies, and {Success Rate}% of customers are satisfied.)

Key:
- Japanese certifications (ISO, Privacy Mark) matter
- Japanese partnerships (if any) are golden
- Numbers build credibility (but humbly)

7. SOFT, HUMBLE ASK — Never Direct Request

**NEVER:**
❌ ミーティングしませんか? (Shall we meet?) — too direct
❌ お電話いただけますか? (Can you call me?) — too pushy

**INSTEAD (Extremely Humble):**

✅ もしよろしければ、一度お話をお聞かせいただく機会をいただけないでしょうか。

(If it's acceptable to you, might it be possible to receive an opportunity to listen to your thoughts?)

✅ ご多用中、誠に恐縮でございますが、30分ほどお時間をいただけますと幸いでございます。

(I am extremely sorry to impose during your busy schedule, but I would be grateful if you could spare about 30 minutes.)

✅ 詳細につきまして、直接ご説明の機会を賜れますと幸いです。

(Regarding details, I would be grateful if I could receive an opportunity to explain directly.)

✅ まずは、簡単な資料をお送りさせていただいてもよろしいでしょうか。

(First, might it be acceptable to send you a brief document?)

Key Grammar:
- いただけないでしょうか (Would you not be able to...?) — extreme humility
- 恐縮でございます (I am embarrassed/sorry) — apologize for asking
- 幸いでございます (I would be grateful) — humble wish
- させていただく (humble: "be allowed to")
- よろしいでしょうか (Would it be acceptable?) — seeking permission

8. ACKNOWLEDGE THEIR TIME — Deep Apology

ご多忙のところ、突然のご連絡、誠に申し訳ございません。

(I sincerely apologize for this sudden contact during your busy time.)

お時間を取らせてしまい、大変恐縮でございます。

(I am extremely sorry for taking your time.)

もしご興味をお持ちいただけないようでしたら、どうぞご遠慮なくお断りくださいませ。

(If you are not interested, please feel free to decline without hesitation.)

Key:
- Apologize for their time (multiple times)
- Give them easy out (no pressure)
- Extreme humility

9. PROFESSIONAL CLOSING — Formal, Respectful

何卒よろしくお願い申し上げます。

(I humbly ask for your favor.)

お忙しいところ恐れ入りますが、ご検討いただけますと幸いでございます。

(I'm sorry to trouble you while you're busy, but I would be grateful if you could consider this.)

ご返信をお待ちしております。

(I await your reply.)

10. FOLLOW-UP ETIQUETTE

**If no response after 1 week:**

Subject: 再度のご連絡 — {Original Topic}

先日は、{Topic}に関するご連絡をさせていただきましたが、ご多忙のためお目に留まらなかったかもしれないと思い、再度ご連絡させていただきました。

(The other day, I contacted you regarding {Topic}, but thinking it may not have caught your attention due to your busyness, I am taking the liberty to contact you again.)

もしご興味がございましたら、ぜひご連絡いただけますと幸いです。

(If you are interested, I would be grateful if you could contact me.)

Key:
- Blame busyness (not their ignore)
- Extreme humility
- 1-2 follow-ups max (not persistent like US)

WHAT TO AVOID:

❌ Casual language: "Hi!" or using ください instead of くださいませ
❌ Direct pitch: "Our product will save you money!" — too aggressive
❌ Immediate ask: "Can we meet next week?" — too pushy
❌ No credentials: Just "I work at..." — insufficient
❌ Generic template: "I came across your company" — disrespectful
❌ Using 君 or あなた (informal "you") — offensive
❌ English (if they're Japanese company) — shows lack of effort
❌ No humility: Any hint of arrogance = automatic rejection

KEY KEIGO PATTERNS:

**Humble Forms (謙譲語) — For Your Actions:**
- 申します (to be called)
- 拝見する (to see/read)
- 拝読する (to read)
- 伺う (to hear/visit)
- させていただく (to be allowed to do)
- お送りする (to send)

**Respectful Forms (尊敬語) — For Their Actions:**
- いらっしゃる (to be/come/go)
- おっしゃる (to say)
- ご覧になる (to see)
- お持ちになる (to have)
- なさる (to do)

**Polite Forms (丁寧語):**
- でございます (formal "is")
- いただく (to receive)
- 申し上げる (to say/do — humble)

**Super-Humble Request Forms:**
- いただけないでしょうか (Would you not be able to...?)
- 賜れますと幸いです (I would be grateful if you could bestow...)
- お願い申し上げます (I humbly request)

CULTURAL INSIGHT: Why This Matters

Japanese cold outreach is fundamentally relationship-first:

1. **Hierarchy and respect** — Extreme formality shows respect
2. **Credentials matter** — Who you are, who you've worked with
3. **Indirection** — Never direct pitch; explore possibility
4. **Patience** — Multiple touches before meeting
5. **Humility** — Never arrogant or pushy
6. **Face-saving** — Give them easy out ("feel free to decline")

**Real-world impact:**
- Proper keigo = taken seriously as professional
- Japanese client references = instant credibility
- Warm introduction = 10x higher response rate
- Western-style pitch = immediate delete/ignore

**Why US/Western outreach fails in Japan:**
- Too casual = disrespectful
- Too direct = pushy, offensive
- No credentials = "Who are you?"
- Immediate ask = rude
- English email to Japanese company = lazy

**Success path:**
1. Get warm introduction (best)
2. Research deeply
3. Use perfect keigo
4. Build credentials
5. Soft exploration (not pitch)
6. Multiple gentle touches
7. Patience
