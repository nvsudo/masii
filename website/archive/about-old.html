Why Masii exists.

Something is shifting. A new generation of Indians — in Mumbai, in Melbourne, in Toronto, everywhere — is waking up to what marriage means. They want to choose their life partner. Not shop from a carousel. Not accept an arrangement blindly. Choose.

But they also don't want to be cut off.

They want their parents to be happy. They want cultural compatibility — language, food, values, the rhythm of family life. They want a partner who fits their world AND their family's world. That tension — independent but not cut off — is the most honest description of what millions of Indians feel today.

And nothing serves them well.

The options today either treat marriage like a transaction — resume profiles, pay-to-unlock — or have zero cultural context. They don't know the difference between Jain vegetarian and regular vegetarian, or why gotra matters, or that your mother's opinion is actually important to you. And traditional matchmakers? The model works, but $5,000 to $50,000 means it's only available to a few.

Masii is the third option.

We built an AI matchmaker named Masii — मासी, the maternal aunt who knows everyone and quietly makes the right connections. She has the cultural fluency of a great matchmaker auntie, the memory of a database, and the patience to talk to everyone in the community. She works 24/7. She doesn't forget what you told her. And she doesn't charge you to meet your match.

If Masii's algorithm produces a high-confidence match — two people who genuinely fit on culture, values, lifestyle, and family vibe — the introduction is free. Both people see a summary. Both say yes. They connect. No paywall between people who are meant to meet.

That's the philosophy: if you deserve to meet, nothing should stop you.

We make money from value-add services. Verified income and photos for people who want trust signals. Deep profiling and active search for people who want a concierge experience. But the core match — the introduction between two compatible people — that's free.

Masii is AI, and we're transparent about that. She's not pretending to be human. She's a tool built to do one thing well: find your person. She learns from every match — what works, what doesn't, what people say they want versus what they respond to. She gets better over time.

The goal isn't to replace human matchmakers. It's to make what they do available to everyone — every community, every city, everywhere Indians are building their lives.
