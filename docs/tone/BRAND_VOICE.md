## The "Why" (Indian Diaspora Context)

### WHY WE EXIST:

Yaar, let's be real.

You're out here trying to find your **jodi** (pair) in 2026, and the options are:
1. Swipe apps where mentioning "family values" gets you unmatched faster than you can say "acha beta"
2. Aunty networks where they've already picked out your kids' names before the first date

**Dono hi bekaar hai, boss.** (Both are useless)

---

### THE ACTUAL PROBLEM:

You're navigating two worlds:
- At home: "Beta, shaadi kab karoge?" (When will you get married?)
- On Hinge: *hides 'family-oriented' from bio*

But here's the thing — **apna culture is not baggage. It's bandwidth.**

That uncle who embarrasses you at weddings? He also taught you negotiation.  
That aunty who asks invasive questions? She's also your pattern-matching algorithm for red flags.  
Your mom's "I don't like their family" radar? **She's literally never been wrong.**

---

### WHY THIS MATTERS NOW:

Because you're the generation that:
- Celebrates Diwali in Sydney but still calls home at 3am for Mom's kadhi recipe
- Knows every SRK dialogue but also crushes at your MNC job
- Wants a partner who gets why you can't just "skip" Diwali for a work trip

**Aur koi app ye samajhta hai? Nahi na.** (Does any app understand this? No, right?)

---

### HOW WE DO IT:

We ask the questions that actually matter:

- "Ghar mein kaunsi bhasha boli jaati hai?" (What language is spoken at home?) — because code-switching compatibility is real
- "IPL team?" — dealbreaker territory
- "How many times have you watched 3 Idiots?" — character assessment
- "Rasam-paruppu or sambar?" — if you know, you know
- "Amma's approval: nice-to-have or non-negotiable?" — let's not waste time

We match people who know that:
- "Adjusting" doesn't mean compromising
- Family dinner at 9:30pm is non-negotiable
- A good jodi means your BFFs *and* your parents approve

## Voice Principles

### 1. **Multilingual by Default**
- **Hinglish first** (Hindi-English code-switching)
- **Regional variants:** Tamglish, Punglish, Mallu-style, Gujlish
- Use native script sparingly (Devanagari, Tamil) for emphasis/authenticity
- Never translate everything — code-switching is the point

### 2. **Pop Culture Fluency**
- Bollywood references (SRK, 3 Idiots, DDLJ, Zindagi Na Milegi Dobara)
- Cricket (IPL teams = personality tests)
- Desi food debates (rasam vs sambar, vada pav vs pav bhaji)
- WhatsApp aunty energy (but self-aware)

### 3. **Warm, Not Cringe**
- Like talking to your coolest cousin who also respects elders
- Self-deprecating about desi quirks without mocking culture
- Never patronizing, never "explaining" India to non-Indians
- Insider jokes for insiders

### 4. **Real Talk, No Corporate Speak**
- "Yaar" not "Hey there!"
- "Boss" not "Customer"
- "Bekaar" not "Suboptimal"
- Direct, honest, a little cheeky

### 5. **Family = Feature, Not Bug**
- Own the "my mom's opinion matters" reality
- Reframe tradition as wisdom, not baggage
- Celebrate multi-generational thinking
- No shame in wanting parental approval

- "Code-switching compatibility is real"

- "Your heritage journey"

## Regional Variants

### Hinglish (Default)
- "Yaar, shaadi kab karoge?"
- "Dono hi bekaar hai, boss"
- "Aur koi app ye samajhta hai? Nahi na."

### Tamglish (South India, esp. TN/KA)
- "Dei, when are you getting married da?"
- "Amma's rasam > everything"
- "If you don't know the difference between rasam and sambar, swipe left"

### Punglish (Punjab/North)
- "Oye, teri shaadi kab honi aa?"
- "Butter chicken preference: dealbreaker or green flag?"
- "Viah = family affair, no shortcuts"

### Mallu-style (Kerala)
- "Mone, when is the wedding?"
- "Sadya compatibility test mandatory"
- "If you skip Onam, we can't help you"

### Gujlish (Gujarat)
- "Kem cho? Lagna ni taiyaari chhe?" (How are you? Wedding prep ready?)
- "Dhokla > pizza (this is the hill we die on)"
- "Garba skills: bonus points"

## Dos & Don'ts

### ✅ DO:
- Use native words naturally (shaadi, rishta, jodi, beta)
- Reference specific cultural moments (Diwali, IPL, Onam)
- Be self-aware about desi quirks
- Make insider jokes for insiders
- Celebrate multi-generational wisdom

### ❌ DON'T:
- Translate everything (kills the vibe)
- Mock or stereotype culture
- Use corporate jargon
- Over-explain references (if you know, you know)
- Patronize or "educate" non-Indians about India
